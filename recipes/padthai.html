Vegan Pad Thai

Description

This vegan Pad Thai recipe tastes just like your favourite takeout version, but it’s made without fish sauce or scrambled eggs, making it perfect for plant-based diets! Bonus: you only need 20 minutes to make it from start to finish!

Ingredients

  • Wide flat rice Noodles
  • Avocado Oil
  • Extra firm tofu
  • Tamarind Paste
  • Tamari or low-sodium soy sauce
  • Brown or coconut sugar
  • Rice wine vinegar
  • Chili garlic Sauce
  • Bell pepper
  • Baby portabella mushrooms
  • Lime
  • Bean Sprouts
  • Roaste Peanuts
  • Cilantro

Steps

  1. Prepare the noodles: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over high heat, then add the noodles and cook them according to the package instructions. Drain and return the noodles to the pot.
  2. Cook the tofu: While the noodles boil, heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet set over medium heat. Add the tofu and cook until it’s browned on all sides. Transfer the tofu to a plate.
  3. Make the sauce: Stir together the tamarind paste, tamari or soy sauce, sugar, rice vinegar, and chili garlic sauce in a small bowl or liquid measuring cup.
  4. Cook the Pad Thai: Heat the remaining tablespoon oil in the skillet, this time over medium heat. Add the peppers and mushrooms and cook until softened, about 5 minutes, stirring often. Return the tofu to the skillet, then add the noodles and sauce. Stir to combine and cook for 1 to 2 minutes, or until everything heated through and evenly coated in the sauce.
  5. Finish: Stir in the bean sprouts and lime juice, then turn off the heat. Serve with peanuts and cilantro.
